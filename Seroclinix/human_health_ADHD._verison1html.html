Human Health
ADHD
Stimulant medications such as Ritalin, Adderall, and Concerta are commonly used to treat ADHD. These medications work by increasing the levels of dopamine and norepinephrine in the brain, which can help to improve attention, focus, and impulse control. However, as with any medication, there are potential risks and side effects associated with stimulant use. Common side effects may include decreased appetite, trouble sleeping, and increased anxiety or irritability. More serious potential risks may include high blood pressure, heart problems, and an increased risk of addiction or abuse.
Safety Assurance
ADHD stimulant medications can have side effects such as increased heart rate, elevated blood pressure, and decreased appetite. Monitoring the dosing can help ensure that the medication is being taken safely and that any side effects are addressed promptly.

Efficacy Optimization
ADHD stimulant medications work best when taken at the right dose. Monitoring the dosing can help ensure that the medication is providing maximum benefit and that adjustments can be made if needed.
Misuse Prevention
ADHD stimulant medications are often abused, either by people who have not been prescribed the medication or by those who are prescribed it but take more than the recommended dose. Monitoring the dosing can help detect misuse and prevent potential harm.

Maintaining Compliance
ADHD stimulant medications are typically prescribed to be taken at specific intervals throughout the day. Monitoring the dosing can help ensure that the medication is being taken as prescribed, which can improve treatment outcomes.
